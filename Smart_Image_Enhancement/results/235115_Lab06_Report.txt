QUESTIONS & ANSWERS:

Q1: Why does histogram equalization improve contrast?
A: It redistributes intensities uniformly using CDF mapping,
   spreading concentrated values across full [0,255] range.

Q2: How does gamma affect brightness?
A: Gamma < 1 brightens, Gamma = 1 no change, Gamma > 1 darkens.
   Formula: s = r^gamma (non-linear power law)

Q3: Effect of quantization on quality?
A: Lower bits = fewer levels = visible banding/posterization.
   8-bit=256 levels (good), 4-bit=16 levels (poor), 2-bit=4 levels (severe)

Q4: Which transformation is reversible?
A: Geometric transforms (rotation, translation, shear) are
   reversible with inverse matrices, but interpolation causes small errors.

Q5: How do transforms affect spatial structure?
A: Rotation changes orientation, translation shifts position,
   shearing distorts angles, all may introduce interpolation artifacts.
